William Shakespeare ‚Äî Sonnet 18 (Full)

Shall I compare thee to a summer's day?
Thou art more lovely and more temperate:
Rough winds do shake the darling buds of May,
And summer's lease hath all too short a date:

Sometime too hot the eye of heaven shines,
And often is his gold complexion dimmed;
And every fair from fair sometime declines,
By chance or nature's changing course untrimmed;

But thy eternal summer shall not fade,
Nor lose possession of that fair thou ow'st;
Nor shall Death brag thou wander'st in his shade,
When in eternal lines to time thou grow'st:

So long as men can breathe or eyes can see,
So long lives this, and this gives life to thee.

Robert Frost ‚Äî Stopping by Woods on a Snowy Evening (Full)

Whose woods these are I think I know.
His house is in the village though;
He will not see me stopping here
To watch his woods fill up with snow.

My little horse must think it queer
To stop without a farmhouse near
Between the woods and frozen lake
The darkest evening of the year.

He gives his harness bells a shake
To ask if there is some mistake.
The only other sound's the sweep
Of easy wind and downy flake.

The woods are lovely, dark and deep,
But I have promises to keep,
And miles to go before I sleep,
And miles to go before I sleep.

Letter ‚Äî Invite a Friend to Visit Your Village

12 September 2025
Dhaka, Bangladesh

My dear friend,

I hope you are in good health and spirits. I am writing this letter to invite you to my village during the upcoming school holidays. The village is a peaceful place where life moves slowly and kindly. The mornings are filled with birdsong and the evenings glow with the gentle light of village lamps. We will go boating on the river, stroll through green paddy fields, and taste fresh mangoes and jackfruits. You will meet my family and the neighbors; they are humble and hospitable. I have planned a small cultural program at the community field where you can watch local songs and a puppet show.

Please let me know when you can come. I will arrange travel and welcome you with open arms.

Yours sincerely,
Sakin

Composition ‚Äî A Village Fair (Full)

A village fair is a traditional event that brings life and color to rural communities. Usually held seasonally, the fair becomes a center for trade, entertainment, and socializing. Colorful stalls are set up selling toys, sweets, pottery, and handcrafted items. Food stalls offer local dishes such as pithas, fried snacks, and seasonal fruits. Children laugh on the merry-go-round, puppeteers perform stories passed down for generations, and folk singers fill the air with songs.

The fair also has a social role. Villagers from nearby areas come together, fostering friendship and helping small businesses earn a living. Religious and cultural performances often accompany the fair, and local artisans display their skills. In short, a village fair is not only a source of enjoyment but also a mirror of rural life and culture ‚Äî lively, communal, and full of tradition.

Essay ‚Äî Importance of Education (Full)

Education is the foundation of human progress and the greatest tool for individual and social transformation. It provides knowledge, builds character, and helps develop critical thinking. An educated society is better equipped to tackle challenges such as poverty, disease, and social injustice. Education empowers people to make informed decisions, find jobs, and contribute meaningfully to their communities.

Schools and teachers play an essential role; however, education is not limited to classroom learning. It includes practical skills, moral instruction, and lifelong learning through books, experience, and the community. Governments and societies need to ensure that education is accessible and affordable to all, because only with widespread education can a nation achieve sustainable growth and equal opportunities for its citizens.
